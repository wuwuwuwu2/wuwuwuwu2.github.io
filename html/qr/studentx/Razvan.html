ENG: What does it mean to be queer in a democratic Romania in the 21st century? What does it mean to be a gay student in a state university in post Article 200 Romania?! It means taking risks, assuming, rejecting, categorizing, it means direct or indirect offenses, it means having boldness and respect for your own person and owning who you really are, what you really are without masks or facades, it means having courage or maybe a dose of madness to face homophobes and ultra-religious, conservatives and those who are not able to adapt to democratic life and society in the 21st century. Although I officially came out at 28, I believe that throughout my life I have shown that I am queer. Since high school I have worn rings, bracelets, more or less masculine chains, not caring much about the opinion of others, and facing insults and harassment from fellow high school students. University showed me a different side of young people from different regions. In Timisoara I discovered that young people can be very open-minded to the idea that a gay person can be your dorm roommate, it can be the roommate you sit next to in classes or labs, and that it's not a problem as long as you don't offend the other person's privacy. Of course, after finishing school and entering the workforce, I (re)discovered that the most homophobic people are the ones who show themselves to be religious, who "guard" God's Word and hate their neighbor, showing pharisaism, hatred and blatant discrimination. "People" who being older have an extremely limited thinking directly and indirectly discriminating against younger people , who have a different orientation. But all the less pleasant things I have gone through, have strengthened me, made me immune to all that is negative in others, helped me to discover the real me, who I am both in my privacy and in the public space, without hiding, without trying to wear masks, just because part of society is still living in the Middle Ages, and to be able to say as Ion Minulescu used to say "They hate me for not being like them,/ I love them for not being like me". After my public coming out, I received respect and admiration from many streight people, but also a lot of bullying from some people in the community, these aspects helped me even more to be able to come out as gay myself, because coming out shows strength of character. I urge all of you who are young, at the beginning of your life and career to proudly be who you really are, don't sell yourself a false image just to get ahead in your career, be yourself because only then you will be happy and free as butterflies.
